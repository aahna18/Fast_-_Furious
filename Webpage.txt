<h1 style="color:brown; text-align:center;"><u> Film series by <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gary_Scott_Thompson">Gary Scott Thompson</a> </u></h1>
<p style="font-size:20px;"><b><i>Fast & Furious</i></b> (also known as <b><i>The Fast and the Furious</i></b>) is a media franchise</span> centered on a series of 
action films that are largely concerned with street racing, heists, spies, and family. The franchise also includes short films, a television series, live shows, video 
games and theme park attractions. It is distributed by <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Universal_Pictures" title="An American film production and distribution 
company, owned by Comcast">Universal Pictures</a>.</p>
<p style="font-size:20px;">The <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Fast_and_the_Furious_(2001_film)">first film</a> was released in 2001, which began the 
original tetralogy of films focused on illegal street racing and culminated in the film <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fast_%26_Furious_(2009_film)">Fast & 
Furious (2009)</a>. The series transitioned towards heists and spying with <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fast_Five">Fast Five (2011)</a> and was followed by 
five sequels, with the most recent, <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fast_X">Fast X</a>, set for release in 2023. An eleventh film is planned to conclude the 
series, and the main films are collectively known as <b><i>The Fast Saga</b></i>.</p>
<p style="font-size:20px;">Universal expanded the series to include the spin-off film <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hobbs_%26_Shaw">Fast & Furious Presents: 
Hobbs & Shaw (2019)</a>, while its subsidiary <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DreamWorks_Animation" title="DreamWork Animation LLc is an American animation 
studio that produces animated films and television programs and is subsidary to Universal Pictures">DreamWorks Animation</a> followed this with the animated streaming 
television series <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fast_%26_Furious_Spy_Racers">Fast & Furious Spy Racers</a>. Soundtrack albums have been released for all the 
films, as well as compilation albums containing existing music heard in the films. Two short films that tie into the series have also been released.</p>

		organization led by Owen Shaw, one member of which is Toretto's former lover Letty Ortiz.<br>The film is the final film to be set before the events of 
		Tokyo Drift. Jason Statham appears as Owen's older brother Deckard Shaw in the credits scene, seemingly killing Han, as seen in Tokyo Drift.</li>
	</ul>
	<li><b><i>Furious 7</i> (2015)</b {Release date (U.S.): April 3, 2015}></li>
	<ul>
		<li><i>Main article: <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Furious_7">Furious 7</a></i></li>

		once again.<br>The film is set after the events of Fast & Furious 6 and continues from the ending of Tokyo Drift, with Lucas Black reprising his role as Sean Boswell. It also marks the final 
		appearance of <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paul_Walker" title="Paul Walker is an American actor">Paul Walker</a> as Brian O'Conner, due to his death in 2013.</li>

		<li>Cyberterrorist Cipher coerces Dominic Toretto into working for her and turns him against his team, forcing them to take down Cipher 
		and reunite with him. <br>This is the first film since Tokyo Drift to not feature Paul Walker as Brian O'Conner and 
		<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jordan_Brewster" title="Jordan Brewster is an American actress and model">Jordana Brewster</a> as Mia Toretto. 
		It also marks the final appearance of Dwayne Johnson as Luke Hobbs in the main series.</li>

	<ul>
		<li><i>Main article: <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/F9_(film)">F9</a></i></li>
		<li>Dominic Toretto and his family must stop a world-shattering plot headed by Cipher and Dominic's estranged younger brother Jakob Toretto.<br>The 
		film is set two years after the events of The Fate of the Furious. Jason Statham appears as Deckard Shaw in the mid-credits scene, while Jordana
